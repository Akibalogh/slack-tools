# Deal Stages and Lifecycle Documentation

## Deal Stage Definitions

### 1. Lead
**Description**: Initial contact or interest expressed by a potential customer
**Characteristics**:
- First-time contact through any channel
- Basic contact information collected
- Interest expressed but not yet qualified
- No specific product or solution identified

**Key Activities**:
- Initial outreach (cold calls, emails, social media)
- Lead capture from website, events, or referrals
- Basic qualification questions
- Contact information collection

**Commission Implications**:
- SDR typically receives full commission for lead generation
- No commission split until deal moves to next stage
- High activity weight for initial contact activities

### 2. Qualified Lead
**Description**: Lead that has been assessed and meets basic criteria for potential conversion
**Characteristics**:
- Budget confirmed or estimated
- Decision-making authority identified
- Timeline established
- Pain points or needs identified

**Key Activities**:
- BANT qualification (Budget, Authority, Need, Timeline)
- Discovery calls and needs assessment
- Stakeholder identification
- Initial solution discussion

**Commission Implications**:
- SDR continues to receive commission for qualification activities
- Account Executive may begin involvement
- Commission split begins based on participation

### 3. Opportunity
**Description**: Qualified lead that has progressed to active sales pursuit
**Characteristics**:
- Specific product or solution identified
- Formal sales process initiated
- Multiple stakeholders involved
- Clear next steps defined

**Key Activities**:
- Solution presentation and demos
- Proposal development
- Stakeholder meetings
- Technical evaluation

**Commission Implications**:
- Account Executive takes primary ownership
- Sales Engineer may join for technical aspects
- Commission split based on role and contribution
- Higher activity weights for closing activities

### 4. Proposal
**Description**: Formal proposal submitted to customer with specific terms and pricing
**Characteristics**:
- Detailed solution proposal created
- Pricing and terms defined
- Formal submission to customer
- Response timeline established

**Key Activities**:
- Proposal writing and customization
- Pricing strategy development
- Legal and contract review
- Customer presentation

**Commission Implications**:
- Account Executive leads proposal development
- Sales Engineer provides technical input
- Commission split reflects proposal contribution
- High value for proposal-related activities

### 5. Negotiation
**Description**: Active negotiation of terms, pricing, and contract details
**Characteristics**:
- Customer feedback on proposal
- Terms and conditions discussion
- Pricing adjustments
- Contract modifications

**Key Activities**:
- Negotiation calls and meetings
- Contract revisions
- Pricing adjustments
- Legal discussions

**Commission Implications**:
- Account Executive leads negotiations
- Sales Manager may provide guidance
- Commission split based on negotiation contribution
- Critical stage for final commission determination

### 6. Closed-Won
**Description**: Deal successfully closed with signed contract
**Characteristics**:
- Contract signed and executed
- Payment terms agreed
- Implementation timeline established
- Success metrics defined

**Key Activities**:
- Contract execution
- Implementation planning
- Handoff to delivery team
- Success metrics establishment

**Commission Implications**:
- Full commission distribution based on final contribution
- Account Executive receives primary commission
- Supporting team members receive proportional shares
- Commission paid according to contract terms

### 7. Closed-Lost
**Description**: Deal that did not result in a sale
**Characteristics**:
- Customer chose not to proceed
- Competitive loss
- Budget or timing issues
- No contract signed

**Key Activities**:
- Loss analysis and documentation
- Lessons learned capture
- Follow-up planning
- Relationship maintenance

**Commission Implications**:
- No commission paid
- Activity tracking for future reference
- Learning opportunity for team
- Relationship maintenance for future opportunities

## Deal Stage Transitions

### Progression Rules
1. **Sequential Movement**: Deals typically move through stages in order
2. **Stage Skipping**: Some deals may skip stages (e.g., direct referral to Opportunity)
3. **Regression**: Deals can move backward if requirements change
4. **Stalling**: Deals can remain in a stage for extended periods

### Transition Criteria
- **Lead → Qualified Lead**: BANT criteria met
- **Qualified Lead → Opportunity**: Specific solution identified
- **Opportunity → Proposal**: Customer requests formal proposal
- **Proposal → Negotiation**: Customer provides feedback
- **Negotiation → Closed-Won**: Contract signed
- **Any Stage → Closed-Lost**: Customer decides not to proceed

## Commission Calculation by Stage

### Stage-Based Commission Rates
- **Lead**: 100% to SDR
- **Qualified Lead**: 80% SDR, 20% AE
- **Opportunity**: 60% AE, 30% SDR, 10% SE
- **Proposal**: 70% AE, 20% SDR, 10% SE
- **Negotiation**: 80% AE, 15% SDR, 5% SE
- **Closed-Won**: 85% AE, 10% SDR, 5% SE

### Activity-Based Adjustments
- **High Activity**: +10% commission share
- **Low Activity**: -10% commission share
- **Critical Contribution**: +15% commission share
- **Minimal Contribution**: -15% commission share

## Common Deal Stage Scenarios

### Scenario 1: Fast-Track Deal
- **Pattern**: Lead → Opportunity → Closed-Won (skips Proposal)
- **Characteristics**: Existing relationship, urgent need, simple solution
- **Commission**: Standard rates apply, no penalty for skipping stages

### Scenario 2: Complex Enterprise Deal
- **Pattern**: All stages with extended time in each
- **Characteristics**: Multiple stakeholders, complex requirements, long sales cycle
- **Commission**: Extended team involvement, higher activity weights

### Scenario 3: Competitive Loss
- **Pattern**: Lead → Qualified Lead → Opportunity → Proposal → Closed-Lost
- **Characteristics**: Strong competition, pricing pressure, feature gaps
- **Commission**: No commission paid, but activity tracking for learning

### Scenario 4: Budget Constraint
- **Pattern**: Lead → Qualified Lead → Closed-Lost
- **Characteristics**: No budget, timing issues, organizational changes
- **Commission**: No commission paid, but relationship maintained for future
