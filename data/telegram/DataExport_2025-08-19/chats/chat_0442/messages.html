I just wanted to point out something that may have been misunderstood from your first conversation.

Unlike renBTC, tBTC, or Bitcoin L2s, we do not have a network of nodes that custody the BTC.

As you know, that is the predominant model used to move BTC to other ecosystems.

However, dlcBTC is unique in that our depositors (dlcBTC merchants) maintain self-custody, through a process we call "self-wrapping".

They lock their BTC into a multisig that can only pay out to them. But they don't send their BTC elsewhere
20:05
This is why we're particularly excited about dlcBTC. "Not your keys, not your Bitcoin".

All other products on the market rely on either multisig custody (wBTC, FBTC) or a bridge (tBTC, Merlin, etc.).

In our system, our depositors maintain the only the key to the multisig that can receive BTC. This is enforced through the DLC mechanism, which was added to Bitcoin in Taproot.

Essentially, the depositor moves their BTC into a multisig that they pre-sign with one output condition, such that it can only pay out to them. That pre-signature is on BTC L1 and is backed by the full hashrate of the Bitcoin network
