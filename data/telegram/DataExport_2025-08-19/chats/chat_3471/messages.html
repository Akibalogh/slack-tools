Jeroen Ost
It's about the platform not wanting to take any currency risk from fluctuations between asset used for paying insurance premiums and the capital that is locked to earn yield but which also acts as a backstop in case of loss event (insurance claim payout). So BTC/WBTC of ETH/stETH would be fine, not ETH/BTC or ETH/USDC
08:08
The capital providers who are native bitcoin holders aren't very keen on touching a centralized WBTC, preferably not at all. So in the ideal world, we can avoid WBTC completelyl and perhaps work with the NFT as collateral. The capital is only needed in serious loss events - but in that case we need to be able to convert part of it (and we dont know the exact amount upfront). This could be going through BitGo's WBTC (we're paying a policy buyer, they have less constraints on how the BTC gets to their account in case of a loss event), but ideally it would be possible without. The recipient of the BTC in such a loss event will receive a little bit of BTC from all of the capital providers prorata their share.
