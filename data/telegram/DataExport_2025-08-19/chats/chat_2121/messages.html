iBTC, previously known as dlcBTC, launched in April 2024 as a protocol developed by DLC.Link. The system uses Discreet Log Contracts (DLCs), which are simple smart contracts on Bitcoin that work with oracles to secure users' BTC.
The protocol operates through a 2-of-2 multi-signature wallet system where depositors lock their funds based on predetermined terms. The two keys in the multi-sig are split between the depositor and a network of 15 attestors. For any action to proceed, 10 out of these 15 attestors must agree on the attestation, which is then verified through Chainlink Proof of Reserve.
Under the iBTC protocol terms, when a Depositor transfers BTC to the multi-signature wallet and receives confirmation from the attestors, iBTC tokens are minted. The reverse process occurs for withdrawals: when iBTC is burned and attestors confirm, the original BTC returns to the depositor.
Participation in the protocol requires users, known as Merchants, to complete KYC/KYB procedures, making the BTC deposit and withdrawal process permissioned.
The trusted attestor network includes prominent names in the blockchain space: Hashkey Cloud, DeSpread, Stakin, P2P, Everstake, Chorus One, Validation Cloud, and Kiln. These entities oversee token minting, burning, and fund releases from the multi-signature wallet.
iBTC is natively minted on Arbitrum and Base.
