Hi, @Alexey_Everstake @Anna_Everstake @vadym_everstake, Thanks for your interest in being one of our node operators and providing the security layer of DLC.Link.

I would like to ask for your thoughts about the shared-security systems. We are considering partnering with a staking / re-staking solution such as Eigenlayer or https://symbiotic.fi.

Are you familiar with Symbiotic? Have you have a good experience, any concerns?

There are several potential benefits we see:

We can get other forms of token value to be security on our network, such as ETH. As we have a network which bridges and issues wrapped bitcoin, we have received some feedback that using a non-btc token for security might not be seen as powerful enough.

We will hopefully open up channels for using our tokens as staking across other AVS networks. The more dlcBTC used as staked collateral on other networks, the more activity on our bridge, and thus higher buy-backs and rewards to our node operators.

We can launch staking sooner using a pre-built and trusted solution

Attract more total delegated stakers by being present in the shared-security ecosystem. Buying pressure from more delegated stakers is the primary driver of token price strength.

We are proposing:

Initially, at launch, the staked amount will be exclusively in DLC token. We are finalizing details, but we are open to providing these tokens as a loan, repaid over time by the rewards on the bridge.

Over time, DLC.Link nodes will require at least 25% of the token staked to be in DLC token. This will be configured via the shared-security platform system.

We are still going to cap the maximum number of node operators at 15. We also don’t believe this is going to have a substantial negative impact on the token, as the total amount of DLC token used should remain, but the activity in the bridge. We hope it will have a net positive impact on DLC token.
