gm everyone, you may have seen some chatter on corn in "Risk and project reviews". We just posted the full review to our site. Here's the tl;dr.

Corn is a permissioned rollup that posts data to an implementation of the AnyTrust data availability protocol. There is one member of this committee that is responsible for storing and making data available. Its signing threshold is 1-1 (e.g. the committee member) to produce a data availability certificate that is sent to the SequencerInbox by the sequencer.

It has a centralized sequencer and a centralized validator (aka proposer). The sequencer builds blocks and the proposer submits an updated state root to Ethereum.

Its gas token lives on ethereum as an ERC-20 token. It is locked in an ERC20Bridge escrow contract and is minted on Corn. On Corn, a smart contract known as the Bitcorn OFT manages the 21m supply. Should a user want to acquire BTCN, it sends wBTC or cbBTC to a vault on Ethereum and receives BTCN on corn. The issued BTCN on Corn is backed by wbBTC and cbBTC on Ethereum.

All contracts are upgradeable by a ProxyAdmin, UpgradeExecutor, and/or an Authority multisig. The authority multisig is 2/4 and also has control over the BTCN token.
