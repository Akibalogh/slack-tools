Aki Balogh
In reply to this message
Funds sit in a 2-of-2 between the depositor and our relay network (“attestors”).

We call it “self-wrapping” because it’s a form of self-custody.

Unlike bridges or custodied models, ours is a theft-proof system - BTC is locked using L1, and only the original depositor can ever receive funds. The BTC cannot be redirected
21:07
In reply to this message
Our self-wrapping is similar to Babylon’s self-custody mechanism.

However, in our system, the BTC can’t be slashed.

The only outcome that can happen is that the wrapped BTC is retuned to the original depositor.
