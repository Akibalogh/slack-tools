19:32

Same merchant system as wBTC, but without BitGo centralized custody as a single point of failure. Each merchant mints out of self-custody. (By design, funds are segregated, provably on-chain and impossible to rehypothecate)

Our POV is that trusting a single custodian, or trusting a bridge (collection of nodes) is not safe enough when it comes to BTC.

It's safer to lock BTC on BTC L1, with on-chain proof of reserves, vs. "trust me" backing.

We use a 2-of-2 with a pre-signed condition that lets only the minter receive the BTC. We call this "self-wrapping".

A federated set of institutions (merchants) mint/burn the iBTC. They comprise both the backing (via segregated custody) and the distribution of the product (similar to how wBTC works).
