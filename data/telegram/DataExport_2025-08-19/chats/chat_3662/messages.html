17:04
Aki Balogh
Oh sorry, we can clarify that. The DLC/escrow supports fees. A couple of different options:
1. Deposit fees taken out at loan origination. Ex, the user locks 1 BTC and we take a 0.5% escrow fee for originating the loan via a DLC
2. Interest fees. The interest paid on stablecoin is calculated by the smart contract on the lender side. That smart contract can pay out a portion of the fees to us for enabling that interest to be earned.

This is similar to how staking makes money. For ex, if a particular strategy earns 5% but fees are 1%, then that staking service advertises it as 4%. The face value return is net of fees.
17:05
Interestingly, using escrows also enables any integration to also earn fees from our share. For example, if Chainlink is involved in an integration, we can pay them a referral fee as a subset of our fees. We can pay referral fees to wallets or custodians who originate the liquidity
17:06
For ex ‚Äî this week, we talked to Foundry Digital who is integrating a lending service into their UI, for their institutional miners. But they don't have a way to make fees off of those loans. By integrating us, they can 1) provide decentralized/non-custodial service and 2) generate revenue by taking a cut of our fees
AB
17:26
Aki Balogh
Long answer but TLDR, programmable Bitcoin escrow enables programmable fee structures on all sides
