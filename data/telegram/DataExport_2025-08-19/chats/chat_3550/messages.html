Aki Balogh
Hi Ringo,

With DLCs, the Bitcoin oracle does not fulfill the same role as a validator network such as tBTC. In tBTC, Ren, BTCB, sBTC etc, the Bitcoin is secured by the validator network. This is why they failed: Bitcoiners didn‚Äôt trust them, and they could be hacked. Also this gives rise to regulatory risk since these networks are holding other people‚Äôs money.

In a DLC, the user is locking their own funds into a multisig, which is secured by the entire Bitcoin network. The Bitcoin oracle layer does not know who the parties are. It just has two simple tasks: opening the DLC and closing it based on a smart contract instruction. Since the instruction can be seen on the other chain, there is no doubt as to what the outcome was. In other words, the oracle is like an an extension of the smart contract itself.

Does that make sense? Happy to talk through it with your partner so he/she understands the approach. The differentiation in DLCs is that the user‚Äôs Bitcoin is secured by the Bitcoin network.

Hi Ringo ‚Äî let me clear up a misunderstanding. The Bitcoin oracle opens and closes escrows based on the lender's smart contract output. The smart contract triggers liquidation based on a decentralized price feed oracle system, e.g. Chainlink or Pyth. The user chooses a lender based on whichever company they choose trust, e.g. whichever smart contract and oracle system they feel more confident in taking loans from.

What we're doing is enabling a credit market on Bitcoin without putting the collateral itself at risk. The rest of the system functions in the same manner as today. That's also why it's non-custodial, trustless and a net gain (same functionality, reduction in risk).

I just put together a list of use cases for another VC fund. Sharing here:
14:56
Finance and Settlement

DeFi Protocols: Integration provides the ability to provide a soft-pegged stablecoin or loan backed by Bitcoin collateral secured on-chain

Digital Asset Settlement Engines: An integration with a settlement layer allows the custodian to conditionally move native Bitcoin in their systems without needing to wrap it. Targets: Fireblocks, Cobo, Babble, Silvergate SEN, Cross River Bank, Fraction

Digital Asset Custodians: Integration allows the custodian to provide borrowing and other financial services to their self-custodied Bitcoin clients. Targets: NYDIG, BitGo, Anchorage, Coinbase Custody, MetaMask

Exchanges and OTC Desks: By facilitating trades via escrow, DLC.Link provides immediate Bitcoin finality (vs. 24-48 hour execution times where intermediaries are involved) and eliminates slippage on trades by facilitating escrow (where several percentages can cost millions). Targets: FalconX, Jane Street

Credit Trading Desks: Integration allows the custodian to provide borrowing and other financial services to their self-custodied Bitcoin clients. Targets: BitGo, Coinbase OTC

Traditional Financial Institutions: Integration allows their customers to borrow money without the need to send Bitcoin to XYZ directly. Targets: JP Morgan, BankProv



Other Use Cases

Ordinals trading protocols: Integration enables ordinals to be traded and borrowed against in OpenSea and other smart contract NFT marketplaces.

Leverage Trading Platforms: Allow users to trade on margin with their Bitcoin just by locking it into a multi-sig wallet as one of the signers

Mining Companies: Allow conditional BTC mining reward payouts governed by a secured hash-rate data oracle either on Bitcoin or the Lightning Network
