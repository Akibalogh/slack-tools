OP_CAT is a single new op_code that allows bitcoin script to combine outputs on the stacks together (concatenate) to create a new stack value. It turns out this seemingly small operation adds a ton of new functionality, much of which is still yet to be discovered. It's would be like a swiss-army knife for bitcoin, but inside a bit of a black-box, as it's quite difficult to reason about.

It does seem like we'd get recursive covenants, post-quantum security (hashing functions in Lamport Signatures), true Bitcoin ZKRollups, and more.

But as this opcode is rather hard to proof things about, it might just be too scary of a change to the Bitcoin Script language, and the more conservative members of the community might rather make a small, more predictable and easy to understand change, such as OP_CTV or OP_VAULT. These wouldn't provide all the same functionality of OP_CAT, such as recursive convenants.

The the power of OP_CAT, tech could be build with ZK proof such as STARK which lets one blockchain be a rollup of the other. With this it makes sense you could create an ERC20 token which mirrors bitcoin with ZK proofs trustlessly.

This seems to have lots of potential, but there are risks of powerfully simple systems that are hard to reason about.

--- DLCBTC

dlcBTC has leveraged the DLC concept to provide some of what the more restrictive op codes would allow, such as OP_CTV. dlcBTC uses the DLC concept of pre-signed outcomes and self-custodial vaults to ensure tokens spent can only be later spent how the original sender wants. In our case, that means sent back to the sender.

As always, DLC.Link works to provide the best experience for those who want to put their BTC to work in defi. If OP_CAT were implemented, DLC.Link would find cool new ways to leverage these amazing new features within the user friendly and easy-to-understand ecosystem we have built for our customers
