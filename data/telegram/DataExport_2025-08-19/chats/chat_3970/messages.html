In terms of the difference between atomic swaps and DLCs: this might be a good topic for us to write a blog post on.

A few differences:
- Atomic swaps are just simple swaps, so their functionality is very simple. DLCs escrow Bitcoin and move it based on any smart contract logic
- Atomic swaps are built on HTLCs, which take one parameter — time. The outcome of an atomic swap is either that the swap happens or it does not, or there's a timeout. DLC use PTLCs and support any number of conditional outcomes (right now, we support up to 10,000 outcomes)
- DLCs are guided by off-chain data, e.g. from a smart contract and an oracle network. Atomic swaps are triggered by the state of two blockchains, but cannot enforce conditional logic based on off-chain information (e.g. asset price)

This article also covers it. See "Classification Of Bridges Based On How They Move Assets"
https://blog.li.fi/what-are-blockchain-bridges-and-how-can-we-classify-them-560dc6ec05fa

Basically, Portal blurred the lines a bit when they contrasted atomic swaps with wrapped tokens. Wrapped tokens fall under "Lock & Mint" bridging, whereas atomic swaps are a separate category

As another Lock & Mint approach, the novelty of DLCs vs. wBTC is that we enable the user to "self-wrap" their Bitcoin, so that the user is the custodian of the BTC (in the multisig), vs. custody secured via a centralized entity or a bridge validator network

In terms of the difference between atomic swaps and DLCs: this might be a good topic for us to write a blog post on.

A few differences:
- Atomic swaps are just simple swaps, so their functionality is very simple. DLCs escrow Bitcoin and move it based on any smart contract logic
- Atomic swaps are built on HTLCs, which take one parameter — time. The outcome of an atomic swap is either that the swap happens or it does not, or there's a timeout. DLC use PTLCs and support any number of conditional outcomes (right now, we support up to 10,000 outcomes)
- DLCs are guided by off-chain data, e.g. from a smart contract and an oracle network. Atomic swaps are triggered by the state of two blockchains, but cannot enforce conditional logic based on off-chain information (e.g. asset price)
