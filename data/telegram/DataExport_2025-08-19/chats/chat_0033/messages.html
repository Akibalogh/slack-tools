Aki Balogh
Thanks! Just as a quick recap for the growth team:

We're implementing DLCs as a trustless way for users to deposit BTC with a lending protocol directly. Unlike bridges (tBTC) or wrapped tokens (wBTC), locking into a DLC secures BTC on-chain and not in a separate validator network. If the DLC is hacked, the BTC cannot be stolen.

Here's a 1-minute video on it:
https://www.youtube.com/watch?v=zDGr09uF_gw
16:49
To answer your question ‚Äî within Maple's system, dlcBTC is a synthetic/"self-wrapped" token that represents physical Bitcoin, similar to a RWA. It is redeemable for real BTC, either via a repayment or a liquidation
16:52
Right now, we're just testing it with small amounts of BTC. But we've gotten strong interest from mining pools such as Foundry Digital, who want to make it easy for their miners to take loans, and asset managers who hold BTC and want to offer a yield (e.g. Franklin Templeton)
16:54
To be transparent, the biggest drawback/weakness is that DLCs are new technology and there isn't broad awareness, so we're simultaneously implementing it for the first time while building out that pipeline of potential depositors as we go
16:55
To show you one concrete example: Joe Hoffend, VP Product at Foundry Digital, and I built this revenue projection a few months ago:
https://docs.google.com/spreadsheets/d/14sfOA3463uerGMnafPFhpjkO_2LeJyNFNF3wMrZ9USQ/edit#gid=1555725419

Foundry could offer stablecoin loans through their UI, and miners could lock BTC into DLCs to keep it noncustodial. (Foundry could get a liquidity provision fee for making the referral)
AB
17:15
Aki Balogh
One more comment (and I know I've written a lot already!).

The "dlcBTC" token is just an Eth artifact showing that BTC is locked. Unlike bridges, we ourselves don't have a token. We are more like "Stripe for Bitcoin", so we implement into other protocols. They can use a generic dlcBTC, or something that is branded to them (e.g. "mDlcBtc").

Meaning, we could implement DLCs into Maple's EIP-4626 vault, enabling depositors to lock native BTC with Maple directly.
