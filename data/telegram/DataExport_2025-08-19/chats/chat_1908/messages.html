08:53
we are talking to BitGo and are building a business case for them integrating DLC support
08:53
as you may recall, DLCs enable Bitcoin holders to lock BTC on-chain (vs wrapping or bridging), so that they can take loans or invest it to earn a return while they're hodling
08:54
our thesis is that, since only 2% of Bitcoin is wrapped or bridged, and since DLCs are aligned with the self-custody ethos of Bitcoin holders, DLCs will enable new Bitcoin liquidity to be used — in protocols such as Goldfinch
08:55
first step, in order for this to work on an institutional level, BitGo (and Ledger, etc.) need to support the ability to create DLCs within their Bitcoin wallets
08:55
to get BitGo excited, we're building a business case with them where we approximate the business impact
08:56
so my question to you is: if BitGo had an offering where they could supply Bitcoin deposits, would Goldfinch generally be interested?
08:56
you're built on stablecoin deposits, of course, so I imagine one of two mechanisms
08:57
1. the BTC is deposited into a DLC at BitGo, at which point a lender issues a stablecoin loan, and that stablecoin is deposited into Goldfinch. (in this case, the borrower is subject to FX risk)
08:58
2. or, the BTC is deposited into a DLC at BitGo, where wBTC is lent against it. the wBTC is then deposited into a yield-generating platform
08:59
so two questions, 1. does this sound interesting to you, at least conceptually, and 2. do you think there'd be any interest in Goldfinch accepting wBTC deposits?
