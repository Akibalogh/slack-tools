Hi Jademont - thanks for reading all of my earlier messages. Yes, this is an important question!

Unlike wBTC, which takes manual steps, ours is fully-automated. So in theory we could support permissionless minting.

However, there is an issue that we're apprehensive about, which we call "Abandoned DLCs". In short, what if retail pegs in X amount of BTC, and either 1. loses their private key or 2. trades the dlcBTC away and runs out of money?

In either case, that retail depositor is unable to redeem the underlying BTC. In the case of an adverse market event, liquidity could unexpectedly dry up.

Instead, it's safer if large institutions, with strong security practices and with capital sitting on the sidelines, are the only ones allowed to mint.

Put another way, if someone is holding dlcBTC, their counterparty risk is the composite of all underlying depositors. Thus, we want to make sure those counterparties are as high-quality as possible.
