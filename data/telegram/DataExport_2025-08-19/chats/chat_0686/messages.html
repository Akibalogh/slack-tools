@Kobewu07 - a (somewhat) short summary here

Our Attestors make up our decentralized permissioned network, where we currently have 8 nodes total, and are looking to onboard a few more very shortly. Our node operators are some of the biggest and most trusted names in the business.

A threshold of the Attestors (currently 6 of 8 are required to sign BTC transactions, and the same threshold is required to sign EVM transactions.

The mint flow from BTC to dlcBTC (e.g. on Ethereum mainnet) is pretty simple: (step 1 is aided by our dapp if desired)

1. The user (we often use the term ‚Äòmerchant‚Äô) will sign a PSBT transaction, and send it to the attestor network. The attestors will independently verify that the transaction looks good, and then will broadcast it on the user‚Äôs behalf. This tx will lock their bitcoin into a multisig, requiring both their signature and the BTC threshold sig of the attestor network to be moved later.

2. When the attestors each indepently verify that the locking tx has reached 6 confimrations, they will do a threshold EVM signing to issue the dlcBTC to the user‚Äôs EVM address on Eth!

During the btc redemption flow: (steps 1 and 2 are aided by our dapp if desired)

1. The user (merchant) will burn their desired amount of dlcBTC.
2. The user will then sign a PSBT to withdraw the bitcoin back to themselves, and submit it to our attestors. If the attestors acknowledge that the right amount of dlcBTC has been burned, they will sign their side of the PSBT as well and submit it.

The bitcoin usually appears back in the user‚Äôs wallet within a few confirmations, depending on their wallet
