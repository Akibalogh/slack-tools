High level D2 Finance iBTC Bitcoin Covered Call Strategy

The D2 BTC Covered Call Strategy enables family offices and institutional investors to generate consistent yield from Bitcoin holdings through systematic selling of call options. Investors gain complete Bitcoin exposure, strategically capping extreme upside (eg. above a $100k strike), while monetizing the premiums from retails DeFi leverage demand.

Liquidity
- Bi-weekly liquidity
- Deposits as iBTC

Payoff
The strategy’s payoff structure adapts dynamically:

▫️ Below Strike - Investors earn stable yield (coupons) from option premiums, liquidity incentives, and partner rewards.
▫️Above Strike - Gains are capped in BTC terms but monetized in USDT at a higher rate, balancing upside capture with stable income.

D2s Edge
▫️On-chain incentives enable D2 to price the BTC option better than Deritibit / IBGT with enhanced alpha

Flow
- Collateral is deposited into the covered call vault (self-custody, SMA, third party custodian options) on preferred chain ie. ETH, Base, Canton, etc
- Collateral remains natively held in the smart contract at all times, ensuring institutional-grade security while enabling flexible exposure through wrapped BTC (iBTC)
- This design decouples collateral from strategy execution, allowing users to retain BTC exposure in their preferred wrapper while the core collateral remains verifiably secure on their preferred chain
- Custody of funds remains in smart contract

Management
Active management of BTC options and delta hedging occurs primarily on Berachain/Hyperliquid, where deep liquidity and protocol incentives optimize returns. However, the strategy’s notional can be replicated at 2/3x scale on other chains.

Security
This architecture combines security (D2s audited architecture), flexibility (multichain wrappers), and performance (Berachain-driven alpha), empowering family offices to access enhanced yields without compromising on-chain transparency or asset control.

1. How do I get my investment back if I deposit into the iBTC covered call strategy? What’s the process for withdrawal?

Ans: You can withdraw your investment at Net Asset Value (NAV) using the "iBTC Simple 1 click withdraw" feature.

2. What does “bi-weekly liquidity” mean in practical terms? Can I exit my position every two weeks, and are there any lock-up periods or penalties for early withdrawal?

Ans: "Bi-weekly liquidity" relates to the strategy's operational cycles, called "epochs". You can typically withdraw at the end of each epoch. There are no penalties for withdrawal during the designated phase. Here's the flow:

Funding Phase: The vault accepts deposits. If you've already deposited, no action is needed.
Trading Phase: Deposits and withdrawals are paused. The strategy operates using the vault's funds according to its smart contract parameters.
Withdraw Phase: At the epoch's end, funds (+/- PNL) return to the vault. You can then withdraw at NAV or choose to roll over into the next epoch.
For more detailed information, please refer to the documentation: https://gitbook.d2.finance/protocol/architecture

3. If I use a third-party custodian or SMA, does that change how or when I can redeem my iBTC?

Ans: No, using a third-party custodian or Separately Managed Account (SMA) does not change the process or timing for redeeming your investment from the D2 strategy vault (as described in Q1/Q2).

Separately, if you need to swap iBTC back to native BTC (or vice-versa) outside the strategy, you can use Garden.finance. This allows swaps typically within 10 minutes (current liquidity is ~$100k, but can be increased based on demand).

4. How is the yield generated and distributed to me as a user?

Ans:

Yield is generated by the covered call strategy and is realized in the vault's deposit asset (iBTC in this case).
Similar to tokens like wstETH or deposits in Aave, the value of your vault receipt token (representing your deposit) increases against the base asset (iBTC) as profits are earned.
Yield automatically accumulates within the vault's smart contract.
You receive the yield when you withdraw by redeeming your vault tokens for a larger amount of the underlying asset (iBTC).
The entire process is fully transparent on-chain.
Note: D2 aims to achieve better options pricing and yield compared to traditional venues (like Deribit) by leveraging on-chain incentives.

5. What does it mean that strategy notional can be replicated 2–3x on other chains? Does that mean leveraged exposure, or just deployment flexibility?

Ans: This refers to deployment flexibility.

6. Can we say, if we want there to be a D2-native “strategy notional” product that integrates with other Core ecosystem projects? For example, could I use my iBTC position as collateral in lending, or plug into yield farming protocols, how we can plan out that & what are your thoughts for the same ?

Ans: Regarding integrating the vault position (represented by its receipt token) into the wider DeFi ecosystem:

Currently, there isn't an active secondary market for this specific vault receipt token (as the strategy is not yet deployed).

However, these receipt tokens will be standard ERC20 tokens.

This means they are freely transferable and could potentially be used in other DeFi protocols (lending, yield farming, etc.) we can help with setup based on introductions you make in the core ecosystem.

Example Integration Scenario:

The D2 vault's ERC20 receipt token could potentially serve as collateral on platforms like BIMA to borrow USBD.

This borrowed USBD could then potentially be deposited into BIMA's Native Delta-Neutral Yield Vault (targeting ~25%+ APY).

The receipt token received from the BIMA Yield Vault (sUSBD) might then be usable for further yield trading or as collateral elsewhere (potentially adding ~10% APY).
