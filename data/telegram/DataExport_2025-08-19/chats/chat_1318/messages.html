Jonah
Hey @akibalogh I completely understand that side of things - I think what's important to acknowledge is a couple things:

The openness / transparency actually remains the same here - all trades and transactions still occur on-chain just as they would normally including vesting/unlock schedules being public on token unlocks.

The core differing factor is the on-chain movements and transactions are no longer tied to individuals or entities like they normally would. Team members, VCs, can now execute their token strategies without public scrutiny or unneeded FUD as it all comes from a single proxy address.

As an example, normally, if you sold $1000 for some personal expenses or a VC needed to rebalance their portfolio, it could signal to the market that they don't believe in the project anymore and cause FUD.

Of course, if you wanted to share what you do on-chain you can, but this is merely presenting the optionality for this use-case, it's not black and white.
