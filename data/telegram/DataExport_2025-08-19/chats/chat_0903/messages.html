Sure, I'm happy to answer whatever questions you have.

For the first one, as of now we haven't implemented staking or slashing for malicious actors. Are not operators currently are a hand selected group who are incentivized by direct payments and the potential for future rewards (for example an airdrop). However, we still identify and filter out poor performing or incorrect/ malicious actors through our threshold consensus system, and our our historical monitoring. Basically, if an actor answers differently than the majority, we record that. Because the attesters are actually just signing what is happening on Heath, we can actually have an internal health system which compares signed messages against the east contract to detect early if anyone is acting badly.
05:11

Second question, Yes, it's the same attesters who verify the mint initially and who initiate the burn/ redeem later.

However, given our design, it should not be a security concern that these are the same parties. Because we're using PSBTs and DLCs, the only possible outcome via testers could initiate and broadcast to Bitcoin would be the repayment of the user's Bitcoin. The most malicious thing the attesters could do would be doing nothing. But redirecting or stealing the funds is not a possible outcome
