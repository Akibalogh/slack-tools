ãƒ»Can Zodia Interchange be used in Japan? Does it count as cold custody, and can it mirror BTC to exchanges?
Zodia Interchange is not currently available for use in Japan.
In Japan, only entities that hold a VASP license are allowed to provide custody services.
Since SBI Zodia does not currently hold a VASP license in Japan, it likely cannot offer custody services here.
Zodia Interchange cannot be used in Japan since Zodia does not hold a custody license here.
However, there might be potential if the role of Zodia is replaced by a Japanese CEX like Coincheck.

That said, the structure of Zodia Interchange could potentially be considered a form of cold custody. However, this would need to be explained and assessed by the Financial Services Agency (FSA) and relevant industry associations.


ãƒ»Can Japanese usersâ€™ BTC be mirrored to OKX, Bybit, or Binance?
Currently, Japanese users are not permitted to access or use global CEX platforms such as OKX, Bybit, or Binance.

ãƒ»What restrictions exist around offering BTC staking/yield products to Japanese users?
There are no clearly defined regulations specifically regarding BTC staking in Japan.
However, Japanese CEXs are required to manage over 95% of customer assets in cold wallets.
As of now, BTC staking via platforms like Babylon has not yet been officially classified as cold staking, in contrast to ETH staking, for example.

ãƒ»Can we license BitSafe Vaults software to CoinCheck and operate under their VASP?
This would likely be difficult at this stage.
The main reason is that Japanese CEXs are required to store over 95% of customer assets in cold wallets, and this operational requirement may not be compatible with the current setup.

As mentioned above, Japanese users are currently not allowed to access or use global CEX platforms, regardless of whether they are licensed in BVI or Cayman.
