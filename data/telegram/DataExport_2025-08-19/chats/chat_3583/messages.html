Although Bitcoin is the largest digital asset, it does not support smart contracts. Today, it‚Äôs not possible to take a loan on Bitcoin without first transferring it to a custodian or a bridge. Custodian failures and bridge hacks, however, have led to over $140Bn in losses to date.

DLC.link provides a settlement layer that makes it easy for app developers to integrate native Bitcoin. The platform uses Discreet Log Contracts, an MIT invention that locks Bitcoin into on-chain escrow, from where it can be moved by Ethereum and other smart contract chains. Unlike wrapped forms of Bitcoin, DLCs provide a fully trustless bridge that does not require a separate validator network. Locked Bitcoin is in held in a multisig directly on-chain.

The team is comprised of talent from Twitter and Goldman Sachs. They have 25 pilots spanning CeFi and DeFi and are launching their first wallet and lender integrations this March.
