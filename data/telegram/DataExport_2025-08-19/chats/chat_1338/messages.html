Grace Gui | Delta Labs & CKB
Hi Aki, thanks for the detailed questions.

1. Package service: Typically, our charging model is a monthly retainer fee for Delta Labs, usually $10k per month, which includes consulting, GTM strategy, limited content, partnership expansion, and quality control of external collaborations. In addition, there is a separate budget for Chinese market cooperation, covering media distribution, KOL marketing, online and offline events, etc. The Phase I collaboration plan is mainly for external cooperation in Chinese distribution channels with all fee included (deliverables mentioned in the proposal). The current service fee for Delta Labs is $7000, including $2000 in stablecoins and $5000 in DLC.Link tokens.

2. $10k for 2 Deep Dive Articles: These are long research articles with in-depth analysis, different from normal media PR releases. The price for a single deep dive article generally ranges from $6.5k to $15k, depending on quality and length. Our proposal includes quality control of the articles and efforts to maximize distribution channels. Typically, the price for placing a single media PR article is around $1.5-3k, assuming the project provides the article.

Deep dive examples:
https://www.wublock123.com/index.php?m=content&c=index&a=show&catid=47&id=24487
https://www.chaincatcher.com/article/2123667

PR release examples:
https://www.techflowpost.com/article/detail_18359.html
https://www.techflowpost.com/article/detail_18582.html

3. $20k for KOLs: Specifically, this is $10k per month for onboarding 5 KOLs, ie 2 months for 10 KOLs. Usually, KOLs charge per post (based on campaigns). We prefer to onboard high-quality KOLs who are likely to engage in medium to long-term cooperation, building a matrix for more frequent and diverse promotional collaborations over 2 months. So the guaranteed number of KOLs for the 2 months is 5-10, but it may actually exceed this number. Initially, we will pitch to KOLs, introducing them to DLC.Link and encouraging them to start following the project.

4. First-Time Cooperation Benefits: Since this is our first cooperation and DLC.Link has many upcoming milestones and clear promotional needs in the Chinese market this year, the Phase I proposal offers high cost-effectiveness. This includes big discounts on our consulting fees and external cooperation costs, covering various mainstream channels. The Chinese market mainly relies on paid collaborations (media and KOL), whereas the overseas market relies on earned media. Confirming the narrative, finding the right channel partners, and increasing exposure in the Chinese market is an efficient promotional strategy at this stage.
