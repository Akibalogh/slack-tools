@moonyose ‚Äî as a follow-up to your question, see this email that I recently wrote to a liquid token fund:

Regarding the dlcBTC mechanism, since BTC deposits are secured at the point of minting and only accessible upon unminting, risks such as FTX-style fraud or commingling are made impossible. However, it's important to note that in instances of merchant insolvency, there may be a temporary halt in the redemption of dlcBTC, potentially leading to the transfer of DLC ownership to a bankruptcy court or an external entity.

In such a scenario, dlcBTC might trade at a discount, similar to the USDC depegging incident earlier this year. This situation could motivate investors to buy dlcBTC at a lower price, aiming to redeem it at other merchants or hold it, anticipating that the funds will be unlocked and the peg restored. A critical aspect of this system is that, even if a merchant exits unexpectedly, the underlying BTC is safeguarded against theft or embezzlement. The only risk of loss would be through the actual theft of keys to the DLC deposit wallets, which would constitute a financial crime. In such cases, arbitrageurs might speculate on the likelihood of these wallets being recovered.

We recognize that the caliber of our merchants plays a crucial role in mitigating risks within our network. That's why we're initiating our launch with a selection of high-quality depositors. Furthermore, we're exploring additional measures to enhance risk management, such as establishing reserves, implementing surety bonds or guarantee funds, securing insurance, and reinforcing legal and contractual safeguards.

Our team is currently working on an in-depth article that will elaborate on these aspects. We'll be sure to share a draft with you once it's ready. Also, we're planning to engage a reputable credit risk analysis firm, like Gauntlet, for an independent evaluation of the economic risks associated with the dlcBTC merchant ecosystem.
16:17
Not to be reductive, but the risk then becomes more of an analysis between 1. the risk of lost deposits due to a custodial or bridge failure vs. 2. the risk of a catastrophic loss of confidence due to merchants failing to redeem.
