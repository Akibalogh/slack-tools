A quick download on how liquidity works in DeFi and how it might map to Canton:

You typically need two things:
1. DEX pools, and
2. Lending pools.

On the DEX side, you need either:
– A stable swap pool like CBTC/WBTC (low impermanent loss, good for swapping two BTC variants — Curve excels at this), or
– A volatile pool like CBTC/USDC (higher risk of impermanent loss due to CBTC possibly depegging from BTC).

In stable pools, risk is low, so fees to LPs or market makers like Flowdesk can be lower.
In volatile pools, there’s higher risk so we’d need to pay Flowdesk more to compensate.

Then for lending (like Aave/Compound in DeFi), users borrow USDC against CBTC. But liquidations only work if there’s enough depth in the DEX pool to swap CBTC → USDC with <2% slippage. So Aave requires enough TVL in those DEX pools before listing a new asset like CBTC.

If we want this setup on Canton, we’ll need:
– A deep-enough DEX pool (stable or volatile), and
– A lending protocol that accepts CBTC as collateral and has liquidation infrastructure.

Whether that’s built into the derivatives marketplace or alongside, these are the two key components we’d need.
