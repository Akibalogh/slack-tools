Good questions!

Let me try and answer some of them. First at a high level, keep in mind that the network pays rewards for every CC transfer. So:

> "current deposit" in billings credentials. Does it meens it is locked? Do we have to pay regulary for the licence, what amont ? is it automatic ?
Yes its locked, and it is automatic. When we Issued the credential, we set the following fields in the offer:
- 'reserve' amount of $6k USD worth of CC. This is locked while the credential is active. The fee is paid from this amount, so it will gradually drop.
- 'target reserve amount' is also $6k. _If_ we were to use the built in 'redistribution' mechanism of the Utility app, it would first top up your reserve up to this amount. (We will use a separate mechanism to pay you)
- the billing period, which is every 10 minutes. Billing is automatic, handled by the Utility application. It is set at 10 minutes because that is the ~rewards-blocktime of Canton, and rewards are minted by block for every CC transfer, so this way we can maximize reward rate.
- the fee/day: we set this as 16.4 USD, so that billing the $6k takes 365 days.

> we will see a new line in billings credentials?
No. It just shows 'last billed at' I believe.

> What does the "Top up" button ?
As mentioned partly above, this would deposit more money into the 'reserve', locking it. This would make the repayment period longer, as its no longer 365 days with the 16.4 USD/day. You don't have to worry about this button for now.

> So when we pay thoses credential billings, the holer ( kiln) pay the issuer (ibtc) and as the issuer is a feature app, you will receive extra CC for that transaction too and that is why you will rebate monthly CC for this to the holder (kiln) as an incentive to do this ?
That's correct. Our validator party is a Featured App Provider, so we earn rewards for every CC transfer.

> Here digital asset is the provider for this utility app (UTILITY_APP_OPERATOR_PARTY_ID). So this is the provider digital asset that receive this extra CC for featured app, then rebate to issuer ( ibtc) and also rebate to holder ( kiln) as incentive.
This is partly true: DA is the 'operator' of the Utility, not the 'provider' in this flow, the two are separate concepts. BUT, DA does have a featured app provider, that _we_ pay CC to for using the Utility app, and they earn rewards, which they redistribute.
