hey, so i looked at babylon a bit deeper and this is what i came to:

---

The network receives additional finality guarantees from bitcoin through checkpointing. Validators responsible for posting checkpoints to bitcoin are known as Finality Providers who stake BTC as slashable collateral. Finality providers lock their BTC into a L1 staking contract via the Babylon protocol.

The spending conditions of this contract are restricted through co-signing with a covenant emulator committee - a federation with a 6/9 signing threshold. If a validator double signs a conflicting checkpoint, then the covenant emulator committee can spend the staked BTC to a burn address. This effectively slashes the malicious Finality Provider.

Tl;dr - babylon staking is not directly securing Babylon Genesis. It is only disincentivizing checkpointers from submitting malicious checkpoints.

---

Per their docs, it appears that you do not need to be a babylon genesis validator to be a finality provider. Also, validators who operator babylon genesis are not influenced by BTC staking (e.g. their stake weight is unaffected by it)

The BTC stake is only responsible for disincentiving checkpointers from posting conflicting checkpoints. My understanding is that basically, Babylon Genesis has similar security properties to that of Stacks except that there is an additional incentive against posting the malicious checkpoint via the finality providers who put up BTC as slashable collateral.

My thoughts on this could change but this is what i'm currently coming to after reviewing docs, staking scripts, and the protocol design. I also talked to a few people who understand it pretty well.
