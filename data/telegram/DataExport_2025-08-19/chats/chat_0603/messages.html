Hi @martyhawks ‚Äî our entity (DLC-Link) is a US entity, and we're not subject to SEC or CFTC regulation. This is a bit unusual in crypto, so let me describe it.

We're a SaaS company. We make software that let institutions (called "merchants") lock BTC with themselves to mint dlcBTC.

This is very similar to the wBTC Merchant network that BitGo has run for 4 years, which also supports US-based entities.

In order to become a merchant, CoinList executes KYB (through Fractal) and executes our software license agreement. From thereon, you are able to service customers in jurisdictions where you are licensed to operate in.

To add color, for Scott's benefit as well:

We are a "WBTC 2.0", in that we enable merchants such as CoinList to mint dlcBTC. In our system, however, the BTC is minted from self-custody. There is no reliance on BitGo or any third-party custodian.

To mint dlcBTC, CoinList locks BTC with themselves, using a smart contract mechanism called Discreet Log Contracts (DLCs) that were added to Bitcoin 3 years ago.
