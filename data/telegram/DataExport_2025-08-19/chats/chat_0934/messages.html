Thanks for asking. That‚Äôs a great question. Ideally, custodians and exchanges should not move funds to their hot wallets but instead keep them in a deposit wallet (for exchanges). Unfortunately, projects that use the mirror service also rely on Prime Wallets, which makes it impossible for us to confirm their activity. (custodians)

Given this, it's quite easy to manipulate Total Value Locked (TVL), and there are no on-chain methods to verify TVL accurately. Therefore, we categorize them into two groups:

Basis Trading ‚Äì Cash carry trade (e.g., Ethena)
CeDeFi ‚Äì Projects using custodians, which are impossible for us to verify.
Neither of these categories counts toward on-chain TVL, and their values are excluded from TVL calculations. Since we cannot verify this data on-chain, Proof of Reserves (PoR) is meaningless in this context, especially since there are instances where the custodian is also an investor in the project‚Äîsomething that should be illegal. This situation allows for potential manipulation, as custodians could simply move funds into a deposit wallet and then withdraw them, artificially inflating TVL.

There have been some recent changes that need to be explained.

Initially, our goal was to make the Restaking BTC category exclusively for BTC collateral that is directly tied (not indirectly). However, due to practical limitations, this may not be feasible. As a result, we‚Äôre implementing updates to how we handle projects using LFBTC.

Projects that utilize LFBTC will now also be classified under Restaking BTC (if the LFBTC is restaked). However, instead of tracking these projects through BTC wallets, we will track their LFBTC activity on the respective chains.

This change ensures that we avoid double-counting values and duplicating wallets. Specifically:

FBTC TVL will be tracked as BTC collateral without any double-counting.
The TVL of projects using LFBTC will be tracked through LFBTC wallets, with double-counting marked as true.
This is the same methodology we use for the Liquid Restaking category, where we separately track Eigen and Symbiotic TVL (without double-counting), while also tracking all projects utilizing them (with double-counting marked as true).
üëç

Let me share a specific example to help clarify this.With uniBTC, they essentially offer two products (though technically, it‚Äôs just one). Half of their TVL is restaked using the FBTC team, while the other half is restaked independently. While we can agree that the overall TVL is being restaked, the old rules made this challenging to track without double-counting wallets.For instance, if we relied on BTC wallets, those wallets would be duplicated under FBTC‚Äôs TVL. To address this issue, we‚Äôre introducing the new changes.Under the updated system:uniBTC will have a single adapter and a single listing for this product, categorized as Restaking BTC.The portion restaked via the FBTC team will be tracked using LFBTC wallets.The independently restaked portion will continue to be tracked via their BTC wallets.This approach ensures accurate tracking without duplicating values, while maintaining clarity in categorization.
