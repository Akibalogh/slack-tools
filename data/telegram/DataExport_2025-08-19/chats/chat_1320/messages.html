Yes, one possible strategy is to use BTC as collateral to borrow other assets and then deposit these elsewhere. The profit lies in the difference between the borrow rate and the deposit rate.

Example using wBTC on Arbitrum:

The base asset in the vault is wBTC. The wBTC is deposited on AAVE, and USDC is borrowed against it at a 5% rate. This USDC is then deposited into a USDC lending optimizer vault (for example, https://app.ipor.io/asset-management/arbitrum/usdc), earning 9%. With a loan-to-value (LTV) ratio of 70%, this results in a 2.8% annual percentage rate (APR) on wBTC. The yield can be further increased by having the wBTC vault utilize multiple markets, moving the position with the help of flash loans to where the lowest borrow rates are available. If the USDC borrow rates temporarily rise across all integrated markets above the invest rate, the loan could be repaid and, for example, USDT or ETH could be borrowed and invested instead. Alternatively, the vault could initially follow a diversified strategy (e.g., 50% ETH / 25% USDC / 25% USDT). The possibilities are endless.
