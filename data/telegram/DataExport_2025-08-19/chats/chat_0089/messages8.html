Gains from crypto-assets held for less than a year will pay 28% tax
Taxes on cryptocurrencies are really going forward. The Government determines in the draft State Budget for 2023 some of the new features that shall come in the form of an amendment to the “IRS Code”, namely:

- The main new feature concerns capital gains from crypto assets held for less than a year, such as profits generated by the sale of cryptocurrencies like bitcoin. The regime, which is similar to that of stocks, means that taxpayers must now declare these transactions and pay a rate of 28%.
- If the assets are held for more than one year, those gains are exempted. It is clarified that the holding period of crypto assets acquired before the date of entry into force of the new regime is also counted.
- Still on the Personal Income Tax (IRS), the Government proposes "the taxation of income from operations with crypto assets as business and professional income". This includes other activities, such as cryptocurrency mining and crypto assets issuance.
 
Finally, it should be noted that the government did not stick to taxation of cryptocurrencies. Rather, the document uses the concept of "crypto asset", which is broader and is defined as follows: "Any digital representation of value or rights that can be transferred or stored electronically using distributed recording or similar technology is considered a crypto asset." So, in practical terms, other crypto assets are also covered, such as Tokens or NFT (non-fungible tokens).
