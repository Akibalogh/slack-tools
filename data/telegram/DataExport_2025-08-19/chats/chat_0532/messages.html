1. MPC-TSS Based Wallet Explanation
The wallet is based on MPC-TSS technology, where the private key is split into 3 shards. The shard held by the user is securely stored and used within the MPC Node, only participating in MPC operations. At no point does the shard leave the MPC node during runtime. The private key mentioned in the SDK is for authentication purposes when accessing the WaaS API. The user generates a key pair, and the public key is configured in the Sinohope web console (i.e., API Key), representing the user’s identity. When calling the WaaS API, the user must sign the request with the private key corresponding to the API Key. The server then verifies the signature using the API Key to ensure the request’s authenticity. This is entirely separate from the wallet's private key.

2. Public Key vs. Extended Public Key
Currently, we provide a specific address-related public key, but we do not offer an extended public key. If there is a strong demand, we could consider adding support for it.

3. Ethereum Signature Standards
Ethereum’s specification appends extra string content to the message that needs to be signed, making it impossible to sign transaction hashes directly with this method. However, signing a specific hash can be accomplished via advanced wallet features: Advanced Wallet Features and Advanced APIs.
In such a case, the construction, broadcasting, and tracking of the transaction results are entirely handled by the user, while Sinohope only provides the signature functionality.

Here's the refined workflow for minting dlcBTC, tailored for Sinohope clients:

Follow these steps, and your whale clients can keep their BTC securely in their Ledger wallets while minting dlcBTC using either a single key OR a multisig key setup (if Sinohope is involved in the signing process).

In other words, Sinohope acts as an "agent", facilitating the minting process on our platform while allowing your clients to retain full control over their BTC and private keys.

Minting Workflow:

1. Merchant Onboarding:
Sinohope becomes an official merchant.

2. Whitelisting:
Client A provides their MetaMask EVM address to Sinohope, which is then whitelisted by dlcBTC. This EVM address can be a multisig (such as MetaMask Institutional) or a single-key wallet.

3. Connecting and Minting:
The client connects their Ledger and mints dlcBTC using the whitelisted EVM address. If the whitelisted address is a multisig, both Client A and Sinohope will mint together.

4. BTC Locking:
Client A locks their BTC within their Ledger.

5. Receiving dlcBTC:
Client A receives the minted dlcBTC directly into their wallet address.

Hi @istvanej, Yes, post TGE we will pay you in $DLC on a monthly basis.

We believe that paying you in $DLC at market rates helps mitigate the risk of a low return. You'll receive an annual interest rate of 10%, initially calculated monthly in BTC. For example, if you provide 10 BTC, the annual interest will be 1 BTC, paid monthly as approximately 0.0833 BTC. Once $DLC starts trading and both BTC and $DLC will have floating exchange rates, interest payments will switch to $DLC, calculated at the current market price. For instance, if BTC is valued at $60,000 and $DLC is priced at $0.65, the monthly interest of 0.0833 BTC (worth $5,000) would convert to about 7,692.31 $DLC tokens. This approach allows you to benefit from the potential appreciation of $DLC while earning a consistent interest rate.
