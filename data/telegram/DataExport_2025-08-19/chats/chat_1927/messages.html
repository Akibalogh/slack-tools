DLC-Link, Inc. - DE C-Corp. It's just a regular software company (SaaS). We make software that let institutions lock their BTC with themselves. We never get custody of customer funds, so we're not a VASP, and are not subject to SEC or CFTC. We run a specialized oracle network (DLC oracles), so we're most similar to Chainlink, which is also not subject to SEC or CFTC.

That said, we did recently incorporate a DLC Foundation in Cayman/BVI. The purpose of that Foundation will be launching a $DLC token and managing that economy. The Foundation hires DLC-Link as the DevCo. The Foundation was formed by Carey Olsen and Otonomos (incorporator) and is run by Fiona Brennan from Marfire (Exec Director).

Our counsel is Ben Whitby, formerly head Defi counsel at Qredo. https://www.linkedin.com/in/benjamin-whitby-compliance/
08:42
Revenue for dlcBTC is through mint & burn fees (same as wBTC).

Our fees are 25% cheaper, since our cost model is 100% cheaper. wBTC gets custody, so BitGo has vault fees, deposit insurance ($100Mn per specie coverage) which is quite expensive, etc. We don't have these fees. So, we're able to drop our fees. Currently 25% discount but we can even discount more deeply in the future, as we grow, to prevent any future vampire attacks by new entrants.
08:43
Fees are taken in the DLC contract (an output that the user signs upon minting). The BTC is sent to the DLC Foundation treasury, currently a 2/3 multisig in MPCVault.
