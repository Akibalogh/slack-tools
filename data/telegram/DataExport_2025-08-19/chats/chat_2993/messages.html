Aki Balogh
Hey Gabe! The difference is:

Currently to bring BTC to other ecosystems, people have to wrap it (i.e. send to a centralized custodian like BitGo for wBTC or Cobo for FBTC),

Or they have to bridge it (i.e. send to to a set of validator nodes, like Threshold tBTC, Stacks, BitLayer, etc.).

Instead, dlcBTC is minted from self-custody. Our depositors (institutions and HNW, who qualify as "dlcBTC merchants") 'self-wrap', by locking their BTC with themselves.

They move their BTC into a multisig that can only pay out to them. When they deposit their BTC, they pre-sign their wallet as the only output path.

With dlcBTC, the depositor's BTC cannot be stolen, redirected, or confiscated by a regulator. The BTC is secured on L1 (not an L2 or sidechain).
