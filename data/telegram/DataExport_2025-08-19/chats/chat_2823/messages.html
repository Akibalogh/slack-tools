So there are two different ways to approach making a feed. One is 'Market-Rate' and requires multiple CEX/DEX listings, minimum volume/liquidity, and seasoning to ensure those metrics are maintained.

The other way is by making an 'Exchange-rate' feed which essentially oraclizes the exchange rate and allows that to be brought to other chains and then paired with the base asset ... In you case it looks like dlcBTC would essentially be 1:1, so I wonder if people would be better off just using BTC/USD, until a market feed could be created, similar how a lot see wETH.

Here are the rough requirements of what it would take to create a market-feed: https://docs.google.com/document/d/1Wzb4UJDsFrYLYA122gIaOuWfRMU45acWI8MCIxf9itA/edit#heading=h.12q8ujrfvsbm

We currently support 16 chains, which can be viewed in the dropdown menu on our marketplace in the top left corner: https://market.api3.org/dapis/arbitrum/BTC-USD

During the next month we will probably double the available chains, as we are releasing an upgrade that will allow us to deploy much more efficiently. We will still probably only be on those that are EVM compatible.
