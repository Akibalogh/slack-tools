To enable Defi (e.g. AAVE lending, Symbiotic staking, etc.), we need to set up DEX pools with $TVL. The liquidity enables swaps between the pairs, and lets market makers trade in and out of that pair (i.e. for various arb opportunities).

For ex, on Arbitrum we have a wBTC-dlcBTC Curve pool. However, wBTC doesn't co-incentivize deposits. But the "challenger" wapped Bitcoins can (e.g. cbBTC, tBTC, etc.).

There are two types of pools: stable swap (BTC-BTC) and volatile (ETH-BTC). The stableswap has much lower slippage. So, the lowest slippage, i.e. most valuable pool, is "pairing off" dlcBTC against another wrapped Bitcoin.

cbBTC could choose any wrapped Bitcoin to pair off with. However, Coinbase Ventures' Ryan Yi told me, it's in their interest to partner with a wrapped that is clearly differentiated from theirs. For ex, centralized <> decentralized, or US <> APAC/WW.

That way, for ex, if a client doesn't want to use cbBTC (or can't), they can send them to us, and vice-versa.

The reason this matters is that both sides are co-incentivizing the pools.

For ex, we don't really want to incentivize a wBTC-dlcBTC pool, since half of our awards end up growing wBTC. And after the news, our clients don't want to put any BTC into wBTC.

So it makes sense for us to partner with the firm that we "pair off" against, and co-incentivize to grow the pool together.

Ryan from CB V pointed out, this also means that the winning pair ends up soaking up 80%+ of the liquidity on that chain.

On Base, Zachary wants us to be that pair. That's why he is buying $15Mn of dlcBTC, to put alongside $15Mn of cbBTC.

A $30Mn TVL pool on Base is a huge win, as it unlocks AAVE, Morpho, etc. there. And the deeper the liquidity, the more that the professional traders (e.g. FalconX, Cumberland) can start using it.
