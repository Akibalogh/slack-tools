Why You Shouldn't Pay in Tokens for Listing?

Many founders prefer to pay for listing on exchanges in tokens. For understandable reasons, they believe this is a better offer. However, on the other hand, there are significant risks that the exchange won't directly inform you about:

1. The token price could drop significantly
2. It will be difficult for you to stabilize/pump the price after listing

This could lead to a loss of trust in your coin among your stakeholders because they won't feel the long-term stability. Of course, it also depends on the amount of payment in tokens

In one case, when you pay the exchange, for example, 20k USDT in tokens, there could be additional selling pressure in the days following the listing

In another case, when you pay the exchange 100-200k USDT in tokens... Once, my project paid a tier 1 exchange a large amount in tokens, and after listing, the price "inexplicably" fell for 2 months. After which the founder told me that from now on he would only pay in stables

Naturally, you decide whether to pay in stablecoins or tokens to exchanges where token payment is allowed. But it's important to calculate the risks beforehand and be prepared to top up your MM accounts
