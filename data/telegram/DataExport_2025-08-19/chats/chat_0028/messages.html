1) Options Pricing:

We use the smoothed Bloomberg surface from IBIT to define fair pricing (this provides a better API than Deribit), and then add a volatility spread to enhance returns.

For example, to generate approximately 10% APR on a two-week options structure, we typically need to sell options priced around 40 bps. These options usually correspond to a 7‚Äì10 delta strike, placing them roughly 15‚Äì20% out-of-the-money at recent volatility levels.
20:49
2) Collateral

For a straightforward BTC covered call, the Bitcoin can securely reside on the core chain within our audited architecture. You retain ownership of the vault, and no interaction is required until the option's expiry. At that point, settlement occurs based on the reference price at expiry.

Two Scenarios:

1. BTC Price Below Strike at Expiry

You receive the yield generated by the option, which is sent directly to the contract.

The epoch concludes, allowing users to withdraw their BTC along with the accrued yield, or opt to roll it into the next epoch.

2. BTC Price Above Strike at Expiry

The structure undergoes a "cash settlement."

We calculate the amount of BTC needed to settle the option and transfer it to a sub-vault designated for settling with the counterparty's vault.

Given the appreciation in BTC's value, even though users receive less BTC back, their PnL in USD terms will be substantia fo definition.
20:56
3) hXXI vs Covered call.

The hXXI strategy is a more sophisticated and flexible version of the traditional covered call, requiring active management and complex hedging. While both strategies share foundational similarities, hXXI offers enhanced adaptability in opportunity selection ( as in last epoch traded digital on ETH/BTC spread)

Due to the need for active hedging, especially with substantial positions, hXXI is exclusively executed on the Hype EVM

Looking ahead, we can think to provide indirect exposure to the hXXI strategy on the Core chain through options, leveraging our v4 architecture.

We can do simple test with covered call and see down the line what best fit.

For the covered call strategy, the primary counterparties are HYPE++ and dgnUpside. As call option buyers, they currently hold approximately $3.7 million in collateral designated for settlement.
