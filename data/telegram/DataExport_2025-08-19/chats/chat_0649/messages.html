Alexandre | Byzantine Finance
Our idea so far to enable BTC native stakers to access cross-chain restaking protocol:

- Alice has 10 BTC and wants to use it to secure Babylon consumer chains and Symbiotic AVSs
- She connects her Bitcoin wallet to our dApp and design her restaking strategy (she chooses which Babylon consumer chains and which Symbiotic AVSs she wants to secure)
- She creates the Bitcoin staking tx and stake her BTC using Babylon. From here the 10 BTC are delegated to the Babylon consumer chains Alice has chosen
- Catalog relayers relay Alice staking tx to a Byzantine smart contract on Ethereum (or any L2)
- We verify the tx details (amount, finality providers, and alice address)
- We create a Symbiotic Vault with 10 byzBTC corresponding to 10 BTC
- We restake the byzBTC to the Symbiotic AVSs alice has chosen
- As soon as alice whithdraw her BTC from Babylon, Catalog relayers inform the Symbiotic Vault and it withdraw the byzBTC as well and send the rewards to alice EVM wallet

To discuss with Babylon team:
- If Symbiotic validators are slashed the byzBTC we need to inform the Babylon chain in order to slash the BTC as well. (That part is out of scope of this hackathon and we gonna delve into it post devcon)

By the way @immabeyeet @nsusruth, sorry for the late follow-up, Devcon has been a crazy time for us...

What we built at the Babylon Hacker House really excited the Babylon team and we won a price! Thanks for the support and allowing us to use the catalog spv contracts. It really makes sense to have a Bitcoin Light Client on Ethereum in order to be able to verify the authenticity of some transaction (in our case a staking, unbonding and slashing transaction).

The only issue underlined by the Babylon engineers is the slashing... If a slashing happens on Ethereum, it will be complicated to reflect it on Bitcoin. Why? Because only the finally providers (FPs) can be slashed (when one double sign, his private key is exposed thanks to EOTS cryptography, and we can then activate the slashing path). On Ethereum, it would imply to give a FP key to an Eigen Layer or a Symbiotic operator, but how to get their private key if they misbehave?? That's the question we are trying to solve with the Babylon engineers.

This issue explains why Lombard is custodial and why it's not possible so far to mint a BTC LST in a self-custodial way.

Anyway, I'm sure a solution will be found in the coming months. In the meantime keep us posted of any of your updates on the Catalog and Garden sides!!
