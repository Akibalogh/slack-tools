07:43
Ádám Dániel Dani Puskás
Sure, thank you for asking!

Here’s how our process works, using Hashkey as the Merchant in this example:

- To mint dlcBTC, the Merchant must sign a pre-built Bitcoin deposit transaction. This input does not need to be a Taproot.
- The recipient of this deposit transaction is a multisig address created from the Merchant's Taproot public key, the Attestor group's public key, and an unspendable public key.
https://docs.google.com/document/d/1g_Kr02colKk-dGvtCaZ0Uqep0A3QjCDwA4A0N0nN8uE/edit#bookmark=id.r80lxtm0krhf

- Once this deposit transaction is confirmed on the Bitcoin blockchain, an equivalent amount of dlcBTC is minted for the Merchant.

- To withdraw, the Merchant must burn the amount of dlcBTC they wish to withdraw.
- The Merchant signs a Bitcoin transaction using the UTXO of the multisig address (the previous deposit transaction) as input.
- When the Attestors verify everything is in order, they sign the other half of the multisig and broadcast it to the Bitcoin blockchain.
07:46
In reply to this message
Yes, the minting and burning of dlcBTC occur on the Ethereum blockchain. To mint dlcBTC, Bitcoin collateral must be deposited. The amount of dlcBTC minted corresponds to the Bitcoin deposited. Similarly, for burning dlcBTC, to withdraw Bitcoin from a 'vault,' you must burn dlcBTC. The amount of dlcBTC burned will determine the Bitcoin transaction created to return the Bitcoin to the merchant.
