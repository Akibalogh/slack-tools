1) First of all, if Solv accepts iBTC to mint SolvBTC, then when your users deposit SolvBTC into the Babylon vault, they will automatically receive SolvBTC.BBN. In this case, Solv would need to confirm and burn iBTC to redeem the underlying real BTC for Babylon staking.

2) Additionally, when using SolvBTC for activities such as forming an on-chain trading pair like SolvBTC - SolvBTC.BBN, a similar issue arises. If your SolvBTC is sold to other users and they redeem the underlying asset from Solv with SolvBTC, we would still need to redeem the underlying real BTC using iBTC.

Therefore, if you intend to mint SolvBTC, the best approach is for Solv to provide you with a vault that only accepts iBTC. Solv would then use this iBTC to mint SolvBTC and manage on-chain yield farming activities for you. (This way, it avoids scenarios where SolvBTC enters Babylon, Ethena, or other strategies that require utilizing the underlying real BTC, ensuring that iBTC is not burned.)
