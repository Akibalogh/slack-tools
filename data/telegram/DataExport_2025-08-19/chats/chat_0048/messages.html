That's right.
There are two parts to the CBTC network. One on Canton, one on Bitcoin.

On Canton:
The Attestors form a threshold signing key, by creating a decentralized Canton party.
This decparty does not hold funds. It can mint CBTC through a threshold signing scheme. (And react to CBTC Burn events).
As long as a threshold are online, the system works.

On Bitcoin:
Attestors form a threshold signing entity through FROST signing scheme.
They hold funds in a threshold multisig. Upon CBTC burning, they can send funds back to users, if a threshold of them is online. No individual attestor is able to move funds.
