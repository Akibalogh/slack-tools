Hey @cybersinsloth @metareader This is Jesse, CTO / partner with Aki at DLC.Link. Nice to meet you both!

To add a little more details to the above answers, the BTC-DLC address is actually a BTC multisig transaction between the bidder and the protocol. So sigs from both parties are needed to later move the UTXOs in the multisig. And those two parties have to use a payout transaction matching the outcome of the Attestor, which is programmed to be the winner of the auction. That’s how we minimize / remove trust and still keep things totally L1 Bitcoin

Hi @lorenzonically and Unisat team!

We've been discussing BTC staking products. We are thinking of a "one-click yield" product that we could integrate into a wallet — and I thought of you.

This offering would allow retail users to stake BTC into Unisat, which would be converted to dlcBTC. Then, it would go into Symbiotic LRT vault such as Swell, be used to generate a yield by securing EVM chains, and pay the yield to the user.

This is an alternative to the user staking it into Babylon.
* Babylon has a 7-day redemption period whereas we are able to redeem in 1 hour
* Babylon yields are quite low b/c they secure Cosmos chains. We can deliver higher yield to users via Symbiotic
* The dlcBTC mechanism uses BTC L1 security, and is not a bridge, so user funds remain safe

Would you be potentially interested in integrating such a product into Unisat? We could co-brand or you could whitelabel it, and provide it as an alternative to Babylon staking
