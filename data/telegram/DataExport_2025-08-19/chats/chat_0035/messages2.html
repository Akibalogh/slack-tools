Even if Bitcoin had Based Sequencing I don't think it's an issue because you'd still have a PBS mechanism on the L2.

Specifically, my understanding with Espresso's based sequencing implementation is the L1 validators have the right, but not the obligation to bid for the L2 sequencing. And because L1 miners can guarantee finality of L2 data being posted into L1 they might be able to bid a smidge higher than other bidders who can only give soft pre-confirmations around finality. However, L1 miners still need to pay to bid to be a L2 proposer, they're not just given free MEV. And because it's open and permission-less system, the bid is going to be maxed out by specialized MEV builders who are not L1 miners. This means the primary revenue miners gain from based sequencing is simply being able to assure the roll-up DA will make it into a Bitcoin block. And that may lead to some additional revenue for miners, but not MEV from sequencing transactions which is really the boogey-man in all of this. This 'MEVil' will be confined to the L2 because it is economically re*arded to let it go to L1 Miners. L1 miners will only be able to capture revenue for the services they offer which L2 proposers literally cannot do, which is putting data into blocks, not ordering L2 TX's.

It has become quite common these days to use staking of a different asset to secure something (eg restaking in case of EigenLayer or bitcoin staking in other models). There is a however BIG treshold before this is feasible (eg majority attacks become quite possible if there's not enough restaked or staked). Eg in Bitcoin staking, if you don't have enough staked, your biggest attack vector is someone coming in with 100k BTC and attaking your bitcoin staking protocol.

When I wrote the spiderchain paper (using Bitcoin PoS), this wasn't a thing yet except for EigenLayer so had to do quite some analysis on it.
The biggest issue and elephant in the room not often talked about is yield. You need sufficient yield in order to sustain sufficient stake or restake in order for the protocol to be sustainable. Eigenlayer immediately has the issue the restaking amount is split (although you can aggregate, the question remains how much this will actually happen irl). Ideally you have one big application (or chain) that has network effects and generates sufficient sustainable yield.

Similar to what happens in tradfi, the market has a risk-free rate and demands risk premiums on top of that. The risk free rate is still driven by treasuries (it's not an accident that the Ethereum staking yield is somewhat in the same region as short term treasuries, or around 4-5%) Anything considered more riskier will need higher yields, that's where eventually the market balances out. Any yields higher than the 4-5% of Ethereum staking can be considered your risk premium.

Privacy in software technologies generally falls into two categories: the application layer and the transport layer.

The application layer focuses on user-facing functionalities, data processing, and data security/privacy. In the context of crypto, these categories encompass actions like token transfers, smart contract interactions, and blockchain messages. The transport layer manages communication across networks, including data transmission between devices and systems. It handles routing, addressing, and packet forwarding. In the context of crypto, these actions include all communications between nodes and validators/miners, including RPC calls from a wallet to broadcast a transaction to the broader network. On social media platforms and other traditional websites, this layer also includes all messaging between the client and server.
