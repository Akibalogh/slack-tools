Stacks hash gives you a checkpoint to verify you are syncing with the ‚Äúcorrect‚Äù fork. Correct here means consensus is achieved on that checkpoint, but it doesn‚Äôt mean if the state transition to date is fully correct or not.

Zkp means validating. You are validating the entire batch of blocks, directly. This means there cannot be any invalid state transition due to the nature of ZKPs.

Yeah revenue is $5bn a year.

The bridging aspect has 3 variables and becomes a bit circular in logic;
- How much is staked
- How much is bridged (gives you staked/bridged üòµ
- yield.

Problem is that these are interconnected. how I thought about it is that the market decides the yield (eg Ethereum is 4-5% which is close to short term T-bills or the risk-free market rate). The market balances out the yields to what it finds to be a good risk premium vs the risk free rate (eg if something has 8% yield but is considered risky -> go stake on Eth and get 5% risk-free). So 5% is the minimum you expect in today's market conditions and then it depends on the market's gauge of risk of that bridging protocol.
So the best way to solve for the 3 variables is to take a certain yield (let's say 10% = 5% risk free rate + 5% risk premium). From there, the amount staked will depend on the activity on the chain.
This part you can solve for by looking how much TVL correlates with blockspace (eg if you look at all different L2s & L1s there is a correlation between TVL and blockspace reqs, although this is impacted quite a bit on what's going on in terms of airdrops / different protocols etc). Adding in the available blockspace/TPS in here gives you a certain estimate of revenue for a certain TVL.
From there you can calculate how much will be staked using the 10% (or other estimate) of yield.
